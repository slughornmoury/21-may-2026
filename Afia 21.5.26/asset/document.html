Documents on Liberation War

The Declaration of Independence of Bangladesh (1971) The Declaration of Independence is one of the most important documents in the history of Bangladesh. It formally announced the independence of Bangladesh from Pakistan during the Liberation War.

The 7 March Speech by Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman The struggle this time is the struggle for our freedom; the struggle this time is the struggle for independence. The historic speech delivered on 7 March 1971 at the Racecourse Maidan in Dhaka became a guiding message for the Bengali nation during the Liberation War.

The Mujibnagar Government Proclamation (1971)
This official proclamation established the Provisional Government of Bangladesh, commonly known as the Mujibnagar Government.
The government formally took oath on 17 April 1971.

The Hamoodur Rahman Commission Report
This report was created by the Pakistani government after the war to investigate the military and political failures of Pakistan during the 1971 conflict.

Blood Telegram (1971)
The “Blood Telegram” was a diplomatic message sent by Archer Blood, the U.S. Consul General in Dhaka, criticizing the U.S. government’s response to atrocities in East Pakistan during the war.

Why These Documents Matter
These written documents are essential because they:

  • preserve historical truth
  • explain political decisions
  • record eyewitness and diplomatic perspectives and help future generations understand the origins and impact of the Liberation War.

Together, they form part of the documentary foundation of Bangladesh’s independence history.
