Interview of Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman After Returning to Independent Bangladesh (1972)

After his release from Pakistani imprisonment and return to Bangladesh in January 1972, Bangabandhu gave several important interviews to journalists and international media.
Why It Is Important?
**Reflected on the suffering of the Bengali people
**Spoke about independence, reconstruction, and justice
**Became a major historical record of the birth of Bangladesh Historical Value
These interviews captured the emotions and vision of the leader who guided the independence movement.

Interview of Archer Blood on the 1971 Crisis

Interview of Archer Blood on the 1971 Crisis
Archer Blood, the U.S. Consul General in Dhaka during 1971, later discussed his experiences and opposition to the violence carried out during the war.
Why It Is Important?
**Provided an international diplomatic perspective
**Described the humanitarian catastrophe in East Pakistan
**Connected to the famous “Blood Telegram” Historical Value
His interviews are considered important evidence of global awareness of the atrocities during the war.

Oral Interviews of Freedom Fighters by the Liberation War Museum

Oral Interviews of Freedom Fighters by the Liberation War Museum
The Liberation War Museum in Bangladesh has recorded numerous interviews with freedom fighters, survivors, and witnesses.
Why They Are Important?
**Preserve firsthand memories of 1971
**Document guerrilla warfare experiences
**Record stories of ordinary civilians and women during the war
Topics Covered Training camps
Battles and operations
Refugee experiences
Survival during military attacks
Historical Value
These oral histories are among the most valuable primary sources for researchers and students.

Interview of Anthony Mascarenhas

Anthony Mascarenhas, a Pakistani journalist, later gave interviews explaining how he exposed the violence committed during the war through international reporting.
Why It Is Important?
**Helped inform the international community
**Challenged censorship and propaganda
**Revealed eyewitness observations from conflict zones Historical Value
His reporting and later interviews influenced international opinion about the war.

Interviews of Birangonas (Women Survivors of the War)

Many oral history projects and documentaries include interviews with Birangonas — women who survived violence and trauma during the Liberation War.
Why They Are Important?
**Give voice to survivors often ignored in history
**Highlight the human cost of war
**Preserve stories of resilience and courage
**Common Themes
**Survival during wartime
**Social stigma after the war
** Memory, justice, and recognition
Historical Value
These interviews are essential for understanding the social and humanitarian impact of the Liberation War beyond the battlefield.

Importance of Liberation War Interviews

Interviews and oral histories are powerful historical sources because they preserve: 


                personal experiences 

                emotions 

            eyewitness testimony 

                and human stories that official documents alone cannot fully capture

They help future generations understand not only what happened in 1971, but also how people lived,
suffered, resisted, and survived during the struggle for Bangladesh’s independence.
